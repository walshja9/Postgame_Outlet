What this is

A roster-based, points-denominated power rating for all 32 NFL teams. Every team's rating is expressed in points versus a league-average team (0.0) and is the straight sum of its parts:

Team Rating = QB + Offense (non-QB) + Defense

A rating of +7.0 means that team would be favored by about a touchdown over a perfectly average team on a neutral field.

How the numbers are set

  • QB — the dominant lever. 0.0 ≈ a middling starter (roughly QB16–18). Elite quarterbacks land +5 to +6.5; the worst starters floor around −2.5. Values are expected 2026 value, so injury and availability risk is already priced in.
  • Offense / Defense — the non-QB units, centered at average. Most teams sit within about ±1.0, driven by the 2026 free-agency and draft roster movement.

From ratings to a betting line

The spread model turns two ratings into a projected margin:

my_margin(home) = rating_home − rating_away + HFA
HFA = per-team home-field (default 1.5) + 0.5 if it's a primetime home game

The projected spread is −my_margin (negative = home favored). The edge is market − my_spread; anything 1.5 points or larger is flagged as a spot where my number disagrees with the market.

Honesty & caveats

  • Market spreads come from an unofficial ESPN feed and only firm up close to game week — early-summer lines are placeholders, so don't over-read specific edges yet.
  • If one or two teams dominate the season-long edge list, that usually means my rating on those teams is the outlier — not the market.
  • Teams marked ▲inj had a 2025 finish deflated by an injured or benched starter, so they're weighted toward roster talent rather than last year's record.
