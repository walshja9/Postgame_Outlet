## The case for #1

Seattle grades out as the most complete roster in the league — the rare team with **no below-average unit**. Offense, defense, and quarterback all sit at or above the line, which is why the straight component sum lands them at the top even without an elite-tier QB.

## Quarterback

Sam Darnold is priced as a solid-average starter (+2.0), not a franchise centerpiece. That's the honest read: he played genuinely well down the stretch last year behind a clean pocket. The rating trusts the *situation* — a well-built roster that lifts whoever's under center — more than the name.

## What moved the number

- **Offense (+2.0):** JSN emerging as a true WR1; the run game reshuffled after losing Walker at RB, but the unit is deep.
- **Defense (+2.0):** already strong, and the Fowler addition adds a real pass-rush lever.
- Low roster turnover overall — continuity is a quiet edge preseason.

## Risk

The one soft spot is the QB ceiling. If a title run needs Darnold to *win* a playoff game rather than not lose it, that's the bet the market will fade. But for a full-season rating, the floor here is very high.
