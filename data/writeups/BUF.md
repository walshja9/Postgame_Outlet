## The case near the top

Buffalo's rating is a quarterback story. Josh Allen at +6.5 is the second-highest QB grade in the model, and he drags an otherwise good-not-great roster into the top tier overall.

## Quarterback

Allen (+6.5) is the engine — an MVP-caliber dual threat who props up the whole offense even after real skill-position churn.

## What moved the number

- **Offense (+1.0):** DJ Moore is a genuine WR1 add, and the interior line firmed up with Corbett and Cushenberry. Offsetting losses: Cooks, Davis, Samuel.
- **Defense (−0.5):** slips just below average after losing Bosa and Milano; Chubb, CGJ, and Stone are the counters, but the pass rush is thinner.

## Risk

The below-average defense is the soft spot. If Allen has a merely ordinary year, there's no elite unit to cover for it — this is a top-tier rating built almost entirely on one MVP-level input.
